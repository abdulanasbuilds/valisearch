I’m a checklist person. 

Startups are chaotic. There are a million things to do, and 99% of them don't matter in the first month. This checklist is the 1% that does. 

If you can't check at least 15 of these boxes, your idea is still a "Shadow." Keep working.

### The Human Layer (Customer Discovery)
- [ ] I have spoken to at least 5 strangers who have this problem.
- [ ] They didn't just say "it's cool"—they asked "when can I have it?"
- [ ] I know exactly what their current "messy" workaround is.
- [ ] They have spent money or time trying to fix it before.

### The Market Layer (Economics)
- [ ] I know my SOM (what I can capture in 12 months).
- [ ] I have identified 3 direct competitors.
- [ ] I have identified 1 "Ghost" competitor (like Excel).
- [ ] My target audience is growing, not shrinking.

### The Product Layer (Value Prop)
- [ ] I can explain my "Moment of Magic" in 10 words.
- [ ] I have a "Concierge" MVP plan (doing it manually).
- [ ] I know which feature is the "must-have" vs "nice-to-have."
- [ ] I have a risk-reversal guarantee in mind.

### The Growth Layer (Distribution)
- [ ] I know where my audience hangs out online (specifically).
- [ ] I have a 30-day plan to get my first 100 users.
- [ ] I have 5 viral hooks ready to test on social.
- [ ] I know one "Leverage Play" I can use to scale.

### The Execution Layer (Scaling)
- [ ] I have a target revenue goal for Month 12.
- [ ] I know what I need to systemize first.
- [ ] I have identified the #1 risk that could kill this project.
- [ ] I know my unfair advantage.
