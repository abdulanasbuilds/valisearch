"I have no competitors."

Whenever I hear a founder say this, I wince. It usually means one of two things: 
1. There is no market for the idea (nobody cares enough to compete).
2. The founder is blind to how their customers are actually solving the problem right now.

In 2026, your competition isn't just the other AI tool with a dark-mode landing page. It's the "dirty" workarounds people have been using for a decade.

### 1. The Google 'Negative' Hack

Go to Google and search for: *"Alternative to [Popular Tool in your space]"*. 

But here is the trick: scroll past the first page of ads. The real competitors are the ones appearing in Reddit threads, G2 reviews, and niche forums. 

Look for people saying: *"I switched from [Big Tool] to [Small Tool] because..."* 
That "because" is your competitive wedge.

### 2. The 'Ghost' Competitor: Microsoft Excel

90% of SaaS products are just fancy wrappers for a spreadsheet. 

If your customer is currently using a shared Google Sheet or a physical notebook to solve the problem, *that* is your primary competitor. You aren't competing on features; you're competing on friction. 

Your product has to be 10x better than "free and already familiar."

### 3. The LinkedIn Search Deep-Dive

Go to LinkedIn and search for job titles of your Ideal Customer. Look at their "Skills" or "Featured" sections. They often post about the tools they use.

If you see a specific niche tool mentioned 3 times in 20 profiles, you’ve found a direct competitor that isn't ranking on Google yet.

### 4. Why 'Stealing' is Good

Don't copy features. Copy complaints.

Go to the Twitter/X mentions of your biggest competitor. Search for terms like "frustrated," "bug," "expensive," or "why doesn't it..." 

That is your roadmap. You don't need a better product; you need a more specific one.
