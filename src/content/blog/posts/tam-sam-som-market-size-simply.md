I used to hate the term "TAM SAM SOM." 

It sounds like a corporate chant or a ritual sacrifice to the Gods of Venture Capital. Most founders treat it like a creative writing exercise—they find a massive number in a Gartner report, subtract a few zeros, and call it a day.

But if you’re actually trying to build something that pays your rent, market sizing isn't for investors. It's for you. It’s the difference between "I'm selling water in a desert" and "I'm selling ice to penguins."

Let's break it down into plain English.

### TAM: The "Dream" Number (Total Addressable Market)

TAM is the total demand for your product if every single human who *could* use it actually *did* use it. 

Example: You're building a tool that helps people track their gym workouts. Your TAM is everyone in the world who goes to a gym. 

**The Trap:** Don't use a global TAM of "$100 Billion" if you're only building for iOS users in the UK. That’s a lie. Your TAM is the total revenue you could make if you had 100% of your specific category.

### SAM: The "Reality" Check (Serviceable Addressable Market)

This is the slice of the TAM that you can actually reach with your current product and language.

Example: If your gym app is only in English and requires an iPhone, your SAM isn't "everyone in the gym." It's "English-speaking gym-goers with an iPhone." 

Now we’re getting somewhere. SAM is where you find your competitors. If your SAM is shrinking, your business is dying before it starts.

### SOM: The "Survival" Number (Serviceable Obtainable Market)

This is the most important one. This is the slice of the market you can actually capture in the next 12-24 months. 

Example: How many English-speaking, iPhone-using gym-goers can you realistically reach with a $0 marketing budget and a Twitter account? Maybe 5,000 people. 

If you can't capture your SOM, you can't stay in business. 

### How to Calculate It (The "Dirty" Way)

Don't buy a report. Do a bottom-up calculation.

1. **Price:** How much are you going to charge? ($10/month)
2. **Users:** How many people are *actively* looking for a solution? (Check Reddit members in gym subs)
3. **SOM:** Price x Users you can realistically talk to.

### Why This Matters

If your SOM is too small, you have a hobby. If your SAM is too small, you'll never scale. If your TAM is too small, you're building a feature, not a company.
