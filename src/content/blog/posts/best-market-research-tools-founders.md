I’m obsessed with efficiency. 

Most "market research" is just procrastination. Founders spend weeks reading reports because it makes them feel like they're working, without the risk of actually shipping anything.

If a tool takes more than 10 minutes to give me a "Yes/No" signal, I don't use it. Here is my 2026 minimalist stack for research.

### 1. Perplexity AI (The Search Engine)
Forget Google. It's too cluttered with ads. I use Perplexity to ask: *"What are the biggest complaints about current inventory management tools for plumbers in 2026?"* 

It gives me sourced, cited answers in seconds. It’s my starting line.

### 2. AnswerThePublic (The Intent Map)
I want to know what people are typing into search bars when they’re frustrated. AnswerThePublic shows me the questions people ask starting with "How," "Why," and "Which." 

If people are searching for *"Why is [Competitor] so expensive?"*—that’s my pricing strategy sorted.

### 3. GummySearch (The Reddit Sniper)
Reddit is the best source of raw, unedited truth. GummySearch lets me track specific keywords across hundreds of subreddits. 

When someone posts *"I'm looking for a tool that..."*—I get an alert. It’s like having a direct line to your customers' brains.

### 4. BuiltWith (The Tech Spy)
If I'm building a Shopify app or a Chrome extension, I use BuiltWith to see how many sites are currently using a specific technology. If a competitor has 10,000 installs, the market is validated. If they have 10, the market is probably dead or extremely hard to reach.

### 5. ValiSearch (The Master View)
I'm biased, but I built this for myself. I wanted a way to take all the noise from the tools above and turn it into a structured report. 

ValiSearch doesn't just give you data; it gives you a **Scale Roadmap**. It tells you exactly which tools to use in Phase 1 vs Phase 4 to reach $10k MRR.
