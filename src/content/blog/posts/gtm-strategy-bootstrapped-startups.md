Most Go-To-Market (GTM) plans are just wishful thinking. 

*"We'll post on social media and people will find us."*
Spoiler: They won't. 

In 2026, the algorithm is a pay-to-play game. If you're a bootstrapped solo founder, you can't outspend the big players. But you can out-maneuver them.

### 1. The 'Sniper' Method
Instead of blasting your message to everyone, find the "watering holes" where your ICP gathers. 

Is it a specific Discord server? A niche newsletter? A specialized job board? 
Don't just join. Provide value for 2 weeks before you even mention your product. Be the "expert" who helps people for free. By the time you drop a link, they’ll be asking for it.

### 2. Build in Public (The Compound Interest of Growth)
Every feature you ship is content. Every bug you fix is a story. 

When you share your journey on X or LinkedIn, you aren't just "marketing." You are building a moat. People buy from humans, not faceless logos. Your first 100 users should feel like they are part of your team.

### 3. The 'Side-Project' Marketing
Build a free tool that solves 1% of the problem. 

If you're building a fitness app, create a free "Calorie Deficit Calculator" that ranks #1 on Google. At the bottom, put a link to your full app. This is how you get free traffic forever.

### 4. Leverage Plays
Find partners who have your audience but aren't your competitors. 

If you build an accounting tool, partner with a law firm that helps people register their companies. Give their clients 3 months free. It’s a win-win-win.
